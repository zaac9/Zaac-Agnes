Context

The purpose of this experience design intervention was to find an experience design opportunity and propose an implementable solution that bridges business and design. Ultimately, the final form of the intervention was a website redesign that brings forth the Italian boutique product suppliers that are present in Italist's business model.

About Italist

Italist is an online luxury fashion marketplace based in Los Angeles that provides a unique value to customers by partnering with Italian boutiques which allows them to offer a more unique selection of brands and products.

Italist claims that visiting the Italist website is like having a tour of Italy's best boutiques, but without the jet lag, the cost of a plane and hotel, or the footwork of going from store to store in search of the perfect item at the best price.

Brand Value Pillars

After researching Italist's brand and reading how customers perceive the company, the team extracted three brand pillars that guided the design intervention:

  1. Small Boutique Partners: Italist partners with small, Italian boutiques to provide authentic and prsitine luxury goods.
  2. Luxury Discovery: Global customers have access to over 1,500 international luxury brands carefully curated by the Italist team and their partner boutiques.
  3. Shop Italian from America: Italist provides greater international reach for their partnered boutiques, allowing international customers to feel as if they are shopping in Italy.

Research Insights

I led the team through the primary (User testing and interviews) and secondary research (academic papers, online articles) throughout the project which built the framework for the content design of the website redesign.

Primary Research

Value Proposition

Problem Statement

Italist’s customers are not being served meaningful content about the Italian boutiques because the content is hidden between sale banners and hundreds of popular brands. Our research identified that luxury fashion customers seek an emotional connection to the brands and people they are purchasing from.

Value to Customer

Italist’s customers are able to discover unique up and coming fashion items through Italian boutiques while supporting businesses with similar values.

Value to Business

Help to transition customers from being satisfied to being fully connected to the business by appealing to the emotional aspect of connecting with smaller boutique buyers.

Design Intervention

Content Design

The content we chose to better surface on the Italist website was displaying the partnerships with Italian boutiques, which we identified as Italist's unique selling point. The content design was separated into why, how, and what.

  • Why: Aim to better connect customers with the brands and boutiques to build familiarity which can help inform purchasing decisions customers make online.
  • How: Allow customers to discover and learn from the boutiques they are purchasing from and browse relevent products from brands they are unfamilar with.
  • What: Customers are able to explore products offered from particular Italian boutiques that often have a very distinct aesthetic in their product selection which differentiates them from other boutiques.
