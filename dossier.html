Context

Overview

Railyard Lab is an internship run by the branding and packaging company behind Mikes Hard Lemonade and the Little Potato Company among other household names. I collaborated with business and design students to develop a successful branding strategy for two clients in the social impact space.

Currently, I am unable to showcase the project deliverables online until the companies we worked with release the final designs. I would be happy to talk more about my internship experience and show the produced designs in an interview.

Mentorship

I was very fortunate to be mentored by senior designers at Dossier including Steve, who taught me much of the branding and creative strategy process. Steve helped me effectively transfer client ideas into concrete design directions to visualize the brand for the client. Additionally, I gained insight in how to communicate design in a concise manner to non-designers using mockups, colour theory, and design rationale.

Brand Identities

Indigenous-led Apparel Company

An Indigenous company with the objective of creating a thought-provoking apparel brand with proceeds funding Indigenous non-profits, communities, and nature programs.

One of my primary roles was to create a unique logo that encompassed the thought-provoking nature that defines the brand while being visually appealing to a wide audience. I also had a heavy hand in defining the values that define how the brand behaves and communicates with their audience.

International Financial Consulting Company

An international financial consulting firm seeking a rebrand that better aligns with their core strenths and differentiates themselves in the financial sector.

I designed three creative strategy directions including moodboards and colour palette options for the brand. Furthermore, I helped to materialize the brand values into actionable phrases which directed the ideation of renaming the client's business.

Takeaways

This internship was an invaluable experience that taught me far more about the design process compared to the classroom. Client presenations and interactions, quickly developing team interactions, and explaining style guidelines for non-designers to understand are learnings that cannot be taught in a classroom.
