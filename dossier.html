    • Railyard Lab is an internship program run by Dossier Creative, the branding and packaging design agency behind Mike's Hard Lemonade, Portofino Bakery, Naked Grape Wines, and other household brands.

    During this internship, I collaborated with business and design students to develop successful branding strategies for two clients in the social impact space. Feel free to check out more details on the Railyard Website.

    Overview

    01. Financial Client
    02. Indigenous Client
    03. Reflection

    01. Financial Client

    An International Financial consulting firm seeking a rebrand to better align with their core values and unique perspective within the financial space.

    We helped to strengthen their brand strategy and give them a fresh visual design that separates them from other finance firms.

    The result of our work is a redesigned website incorporating our brand strategy, which can be found at Momentus.global.

    My Roles

    Brand Strategy

    A primary role of mine was to create the core essence of the brand, including the attributes and characteristics it embodies.

    One of the major goals with the brand strategy was to position them apart from other financial clients and use relevant, real examples to illustrate their global impact.

    Creative Strategy

    I co-created the visual strategy the brand ultimately went forth with in their rebrand.

    We designed three options for the client, each emphasizing different aspects of their core values.

    02. Indigenous Client

    An Indigenous educational non-profit with the goal of transforming the way nature conservation occurs in Canada.

    They came to us to create a thought-provoking apparel brand to support Indigenous led non-profit communities and nature preservation initiatives such as IPCAs.

    My Roles

    Brand Strategy

    I also created the core essence of the brand for this client, including the attributes and characteristics it embodies, as well as define how the brand speaks to its audience.

    The initial research and brand strategy laid the foundation for the remainder of the project and helped to shape the visual design work into the final form.

    Logo Design

    I designed the logo that the brand went forward with. The logo is designed to be diverse in that it could be implemented at very small or extremely large scale.

    Furthermore, the logo has ties to Indigenous culture while also working as a graphic that could be worn in a business-casual environment for customers looking for an every-day look.

    Creative Strategy and Photo editing

    I assisted with the creative strategy to ensure it aligned with the brand strategy set forth.

    Additionally, I later used photoshop and other deisgn tools to create mockups of what the brand would ultimately look like in the hands of consumers.

    03. Reflection

    This internship was an invaluable experience that taught me much of the design process including working with clients, developing co-worker dynamics, and creating style guides that are easy for non-designers to follow.

    I also had the pleasure of working closely with senior designers and project managers and gained insight to working in an agency setting.

    I am happy to share more details and the designs of the projects I worked on in person or over a call.
