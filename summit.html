    • For a senior user interface design project, we prototyped an application that gives skiers an opportunity to share live trail information, weather conditions, and recommendations while on the mountain.

    The goal of the mobile application is to inform others of current conditions and hazards, as well as recommended trails.

    01. Final Deliverables

    02. Understanding Our Users

    Competition Space

    I led the team through a landscape research of popular ski trail applications on the market to discover where was an opportunity for an application.

    I discovered there was a gap in the market of user-generated reviews and community engagement.

    UX Research

    Our goal with Summit is to be beneficial for all levels of skiers and snowboarders, so we conducted primary and secondary research with a wide range of users.

    We discovered that many beginners struggle to navigate unfamiliar terrain, while experts are seeking challenging runs to push their skills.

    03. Ideation

    Rough Sketches

    When designing the initial user interface, I had the insight to emphasize using larger icons and buttons to account for cold fingers or touchscreen gloves while on the mountain.

    Users can later go back and add a more detailed review when they have warmed up.

    High Fidelty Prototype

    I designed the iconography, copywriting, user flow, and the review section of the user interface.

    We wanted this process to be a quick as to not take away from time on the mountain, while providing the necessary functions we identified in our research.

    04. User Testing

    Key Tasks

    We conducted user tests online as to maintain safety during Covid-19. My responsibility was to give the participants tasks to complete in the application.

    We let the users try to complete the tasks on their own and only provided assistant if it was requested.

    Surveys and Interviews

    After performing the tasks, we held a brief interview where we asked open-ended questions about the experience using the application.

    Some of the participants stated they search for trail reviews online, so a centralized application would make the process much easier. One interviewee mentioned they use a similar app for hiking, so using one for skiing would make sense and be intuitive.

    Revisions

    A major insight that we became aware of during testing was the functionality of the application was not immediately clear to new users.

    Thus, a quick tutorial was added to help guide new users through the features of the application.

    05. Reflection

    Through the process of prototyping an application from start to finish I learned the importance of empathizing with a particular user base through various design methods. For instance, gathering sufficient preliminary research along with performing user testing early in the design process is crucial to designing a successful product for the user.

    Embracing teamwork early on and distributing the workload amongst team members ultimately had a major role in the development of the application. While we each specialized in individual tasks, we continued to maintain unity in the deisgn of the application by constantly checking in with one another.
